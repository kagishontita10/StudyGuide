Faculty of Humanities

Exploring culture, thought, language, and the human experience.

Why Study Humanities?

Humanities help us understand the world through history, philosophy, language, and the arts. They foster critical thinking, empathy, and cultural awareness.

Humanities Disciplines

Philosophy

Explores fundamental questions about existence, ethics, logic, and human reasoning.

Career Paths: Ethicist, lecturer, policy advisor, writer, researcher.

Key Subjects & Entry Requirements

  • English, History, Life Orientation
  • Minimum APS and reading proficiency
  • Strong analytical and reflective thinking

History

Studies past events, societies, and cultures to understand present realities and future possibilities.

Career Paths: Historian, archivist, museum curator, educator, journalist.

Key Subjects & Entry Requirements

  • English, History, Life Orientation
  • Minimum APS and reading proficiency
  • Strong analytical and reflective thinking

Linguistics

Analyzes language structure, evolution, and usage across cultures and contexts.

Career Paths: Translator, language analyst, speech therapist, lexicographer.

Key Subjects & Entry Requirements

  • English, History, Life Orientation
  • Minimum APS and reading proficiency
  • Strong analytical and reflective thinking

General Entry Requirements

To study Humanities, students typically need:

  • Strong performance in English and History
  • Interest in culture, ideas, and human behavior
  • Critical thinking and communication skills
