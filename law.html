Faculty of Law

Upholding justice, shaping policy, and defending human rights.

Why Study Law?

Law is the foundation of civil society. It governs relationships, protects rights, and ensures justice. Studying law equips you to interpret, apply, and shape legal systems.

Legal Disciplines

Criminal Law

Focuses on offenses against the state and society, including prosecution and defense.

Career Paths: Prosecutor, defense attorney, criminal investigator, judge.

Key Subjects & Entry Requirements

  • English, History, Life Orientation
  • Minimum APS and language proficiency
  • Strong analytical and ethical reasoning

Civil Law

Deals with disputes between individuals or organizations, including contracts and property.

Career Paths: Legal advisor, mediator, corporate lawyer, civil litigator.

Key Subjects & Entry Requirements

  • English, History, Life Orientation
  • Minimum APS and language proficiency
  • Strong analytical and ethical reasoning

Constitutional Law

Explores the rights and duties enshrined in a nation's constitution and legal framework.

Career Paths: Human rights lawyer, policy analyst, legal scholar, government advisor.

Key Subjects & Entry Requirements

  • English, History, Life Orientation
  • Minimum APS and language proficiency
  • Strong analytical and ethical reasoning

General Entry Requirements

To study Law, students typically need:

  • Strong performance in English and History
  • Excellent communication and critical thinking skills
  • Passion for justice, fairness, and public service
