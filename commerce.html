Faculty of Commerce

Driving business, finance, and economic innovation across industries.

Why Study Commerce?

Commerce equips students with the tools to understand markets, manage resources, and lead organizations. It blends analytical thinking with strategic decision-making.

Commerce Specializations

Accounting

Focuses on financial reporting, auditing, and budgeting for individuals and organizations.

Career Paths: Chartered accountant, auditor, tax consultant, financial controller.

Key Subjects & Entry Requirements

  • Mathematics, Accounting, Business Studies
  • Minimum APS and numeracy skills
  • Attention to detail and ethical integrity

Marketing

Explores consumer behavior, branding, advertising, and digital strategy to drive engagement and sales.

Career Paths: Marketing manager, brand strategist, digital marketer, market researcher.

Key Subjects & Entry Requirements

  • English, Business Studies, Economics
  • Minimum APS and creativity
  • Strong communication and analytical skills

Economics

Analyzes production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services within societies.

Career Paths: Economist, policy analyst, financial advisor, investment strategist.

Key Subjects & Entry Requirements

  • Mathematics, Economics, Business Studies
  • Minimum APS and analytical thinking
  • Interest in global trends and data interpretation

General Entry Requirements

To study Commerce, students typically need:

  • Strong performance in Mathematics and Business-related subjects
  • Interest in finance, strategy, and entrepreneurship
  • Numerical accuracy and communication skills
